## Why the H4 sample is too small for deployment

91 trades over 6 years = ~15 trades per year. With 6 years of data, that's a thin sample for any statistical inference:

- 95% CI on win rate: ±10 percentage points (true rate could be 36-56%)
- One bad trade-cluster in OOS can drop PF from 3.0 to 1.5
- A single 6-year regime (gold's 2020-2026 secular bull) generates basically *one* large trend cluster — H4's wins are likely concentrated in 2-3 clusters

For the same underlying strategy at H1 (368 trades, ~60/year), confidence intervals are 2-3× tighter and clusters average out.

## What this confirms about H1

The H1 result is NOT cherry-picked or regime-fragile. Two adjacent timeframes show the same edge shape:
- H4 shows the SAME asymmetric structure scaled up
- M15 shows the SAME asymmetric structure scaled down (just the win rate dilution drops it below PF gate)

A fragile / overfit strategy would collapse in BOTH directions or show inverted shape (better at M15 worse at H1, or random noise). This shows clean, monotonic shape — that's a real edge.

## Recommendation

1. **Deploy H1 only.** Don't deploy H4 (trade count) or M15 (PF gate).
2. **Use H4 + M15 as triangulation.** When QuantDash + IS/OOS validate H1, the H4 result becomes additional evidence that the H1 numbers aren't a single-window fluke.
3. **Don't optimize H4 by widening parameters.** Tempting (PF 3 looks great) but with 91 trades any "optimization" is overfitting to <10 actual edge clusters.

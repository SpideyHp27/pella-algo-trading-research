## What this tells us

The strategy's core mechanic (asymmetric Donchian/Keltner channels) **does survive** at M15 — it stays profitable with the same parameters and the asymmetry actually holds up. What collapses is the **win rate**: 40% → 32%. Translation: M15 produces more false breakouts that immediately get exited, but when wins happen they're proportionally still 2.5× the losses.

This is the textbook signature of a **slow-signal strategy on too-fast a timeframe**. The 20/40-bar Donchian on M15 looks back ~5/10 hours. On H1 the same lookback is ~20/40 hours — covers a real swing. M15 catches noise that H1 filters out.

**Per the Pipeline v1.3 rule:** "if H1 works, M30 and H2 should not collapse." M15 is two TFs below H1 (skip M30). Strategy is still positive (doesn't collapse to negative), but falls below gate — a **soft warning**, not a fail of the H1 result.

## Possible re-tuning paths (do NOT implement without spec)

If we want this to work on M15 specifically (not required — H1 is fine):

1. **Stretch lookbacks 4×:** DonchianUpper=80, DonchianLower=160, KeltnerEMA=80, KeltnerLong=160 — equivalent bar-count to H1 defaults.
2. **Higher AtrMultiplier:** 1.4 → 2.0 widens the Keltner band, filters out more chop.
3. **Add a trend filter:** require price > 200-period MA on M15 OR > 50-period MA on H1 (cross-TF confirmation).

Better instinct: **don't optimize for M15.** This data point validates that H1 is the natural fit.
